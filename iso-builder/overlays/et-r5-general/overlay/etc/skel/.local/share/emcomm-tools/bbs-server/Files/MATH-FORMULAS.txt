1. Area of Rectangle: area = length x width
Kids will need to know this one in pre-algebra and later math classes. 

2. Perimeter of a rectangle: 2(l + b)
This will calculate the total of the outer lines of a rectangle. 

3. Area of a square: a² 
Square the length of both sides to find the area of a square. 

4. Perimeter of a square: 4 x a
Multiply the length of the side of the square by 4 to find the perimeter. 

5. Area of a circle: π x r²
Use this to find the total area of a circle. 

6. Circumference of a circle: 2π x  r
This formula calculates the length of the outside of a circle.

7. Find the Average: Sum of total numbers divided by the number of values. 
Useful in statistics and many more math word problems. 

These math formulas will come in handy in geometry, algebra, calculus and more. 

8. The Quadratic Formula: x = −b ± √b²-4ac/2a
Students will need this to solve quadratic equations in algebra II and beyond. As an added pneumonic bonus, it’s easier to remember this one to the tune of “Pop Goes the Weasel”. 

9. Probability: Number of favorable outcomes divided by the total number of favorable outcomes.
To determine the probability of an event, this equation will determine the odds of something happening. 

10. Distance formula: d=√(x₁ – x₂)² + (y₁ – y₂)²
This equation will calculate the distance between two points, which will be necessary in geometry and algebra.

11. Slope Formula: Slope = y₂ – y₁ /  x₂ – x₁
Use this to find the angle that joins two points on a graph. 

12. Slope Intercept Formula: y=mx+b
In algebra, this will find the slope intercept of a straight line. 

13. Area of Triangle: Area = (1/2) (base) (height)
Use this in geometry to find the total area of a triangle. 

14. Sine (SOH): Sine = opposite / hypotenuse
This equation calculates the unknown angles of a triangle. 

15. Cosine (CAH): Cosine = adjacent / hypotenuse
Use this equation to find the unknown angles of a triangle. 

16. Tangent (TOA): Tangent = opposite / adjacent
This equation also can be used to calculate the unknown angles of a triangle. 

17. The Pythagorean Theorem: a²+b²=c²
This is used to find the length of a hypotenuse in a right triangle. In any right triangle, the square of the length of the hypotenuse equals the sum of the squares of the lengths of the legs of the right triangle.

Complex Number Formulas
These complex number formulas are essential for mastering advanced algebra, trigonometry, and geometry. Whether tackling high school math, preparing for the SAT®, or exploring higher-level math concepts, these tools will simplify challenging problems and build a strong foundation.

18. Standard Form of a Complex Number: z = a +bi
Where a is the real part, and b is the imaginary part.

19. Modulus of a Complex Number: |z| = √a²+b²
This equation calculates the distance of the complex number from the origin in the complex plane.

20. Conjugate of a Complex Number: z̄ = a - bi 
This formula is useful in rationalizing denominators or simplifying expressions.

Mensuration Formulas (Essential Geometry Measurements)
Mensuration is an important branch of mathematics that deals with the measurement of different shapes and figures. Whether calculating area, perimeter, or volume, these formulas are required for solving real-world problems in geometry.

21. Volume of a Cube: Volume = a³
Use this formula to find the total space occupied by a cube, where a represents the length of a side.

22. Volume of a Cylinder: Volume = π × r² × h
This formula calculates the space inside a cylinder, where r is the radius of the base and h is the height.

23. Surface Area of a Sphere: Surface Area = 4π × r²
Use this to find the total outer surface area of a sphere.
